Shelved

A next.js project bootstrapped with create-next-app.

Digital distribution platforms such as Steam, Epic, Origin, and GOG have transformed how players acquire video games. Not only the most niche of genres but also up to the biggest AAA games. Add to that, the frequent sales and bundles that get thrown into the mix at certain intervals which bring the sudden shopping sprees causing players to own hundreds of games that far exceed the time available to play them.  This abundance creates a backlog of unplayed or unfinished games waiting for attention. 

When confronted with too many choices at once, instead of enabling escape and enjoyment, decision paralysis awaits them. This phenomenon is closely related to the psychological concepts of choice overload and opportunity costs.

Understanding the causes and consequences of decision paralysis is important. Games are meant to be a leisure activity and not an excessive choice that turns playtime into another source of stress or total avoidance. Reduction of decision paralysis will not only allow the gamers to get back their time of fun but will also help the overall gaming community and economy.
